# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Women roughly 25–45 buying herbal feminine wellness pads, most of them for the first time. They arrive skeptical twice over: wary of conventional pharma-adjacent femcare, and equally wary of vague "wellness" marketing. They are researching an alternative they half-hope works, usually on a phone, often from a social ad or a friend's mention. The job to be done: decide within a minute or two whether this brand is credible enough to try once — then find the single product that fits them and buy it without friction.

## Product Purpose

VAÉ Wellness (vaewellness.com) sells feminine wellness pads infused with traditional Chinese medicine herbs (Xue Lian Hua, Bai Zhi, Ding Xiang, Man Shan Hong, Bai Xian Pi). The storefront exists to convert skeptical first-time buyers. Success looks like: a visitor lands, feels reassured within five seconds, understands what the herbs do and why they're safe, and reaches checkout on an individual product without hesitation. Individual products lead the catalog; bundle tiers exist as a supporting offer, never the gate.

## Brand Personality

"The Herbalist's Confidante," evolved. Calm authority leads; intimacy follows. Three words: **credible, warm, unhurried**. The voice is a knowledgeable woman who has used these herbs for years and explains them plainly — never a lab coat, never a bubbly best friend. Emotional goal for the first five seconds: *reassured* — heritage of the herbs, safety, and the guarantee carry the opening, and the woman-to-woman warmth emerges as the page unfolds.

## Anti-references

- **Cutesy period-brand pastel**: bubbly infantilizing pink, cartoon flowers, exclamation-mark copy — the saturated femcare-startup look. VAÉ's rose tones must read deep and grown, never candy.
- **Beige luxury-spa cliché**: whisper-thin serifs on cream, everything beige, "wellness" as visual wallpaper with no point of view. VAÉ commits to saturated color and typography with real weight.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust before intimacy.** Proof, herb heritage, and the guarantee appear before poetry. Every claim is anchored to a named herb or a concrete policy.
2. **The herbs are the heroes.** Name them, show them, explain what each does. Botanical specificity is the differentiator against both pharma and generic wellness.
3. **Calm authority.** Generous space, deliberate pacing, no countdown timers, no fake scarcity, no urgency tricks. Reassurance is the conversion strategy.
4. **Individual products lead.** The catalog and homepage sell single products first; bundles are a supporting offer placed after trust is established.
5. **Motion is a whisper.** Subtle drift, soft light, slow loops. Never spectacle; always a reduced-motion fallback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast against its background (including on the deep mulberry surfaces), large text ≥3:1, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, alt text on all imagery, and `prefers-reduced-motion` fallbacks — the video hero degrades to a still frame.
